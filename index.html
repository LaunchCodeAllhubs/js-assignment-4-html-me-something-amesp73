Prior to Adoption

Prior to Blair, I had known for quite a while I wanted to adopt a dog. I had grown up with dogs all my life and have always been a huge animal lover, with an extra special soft spot for dogs. On my hunt for a pet, I knew I didn't want to adopt carelessly, I wanted to adopt for life. After hours upon hours of searching many websites and shelters, I saw the photos you see above. She wasn't the most presentable dog on the website, but something drew me to her. I contacted the shelter immediately and set up a meet and greet with her. I met her at her foster parent's house and was instantly taken with her. She was shy but was desperate to show just how much love she was ready to give and receive. Her foster parent's shared that she was found roaming the streets scavenging for food, and had also appeared to have birthed a litter pretty recently. The litter was nowhere to be found and shelter staff assumed she was potentially used for breeding and dumped. She was also heartworm positive, which sadly meant people were not interested in the possible complications and expenses associated with that. This meant Blair had ZERO interest in her, no one had inquired or applied to even meet her. We will never knew the true story of what her life was, but on that day, I decided it doesn't matter, because every day after that will always be better. I stocked up on supplies and took her home the very next day. Below you can read more about our journey!

Adjusting to Our New Life

Walking out the door with Blair was quite possibly one of the most terrifying things. I almost turned right around and gave her back! The thought of owning a dog was great, but was I actually ready to change my life? Was I ready to adjust every aspect for a dog that I know hardly anything about? Well it was too late. To be honest, it was solely out of pure embarrassment that I didn't turn around. And I'm glad I didn't. Was it an adjustment? Major. I was used to doing things when I wanted to and going places when I wanted to.

Within the first week I learned many things. She was an early riser, cue weekend 6am wake up calls. It was tiring and sometimes I could get her to sleep in, but we mostly just compromised with each other and adjusted.

She was also reactive to every single animal out there. I found that out the hard way on our walks when she almost took me down multiple times over a squirrel, a duck, a deer, you name it. Neighbors would come to say hi and she'd snap at their dogs. We soon started to avoid times when everyone was out walking their dogs. It is not easy making friends with your neighbors when you have a reactive dog!

She was also very dependent. She loved to be around me. If we are on the sofa, she's in my lap. After I shower, she is waiting outside the door. When I cook, she's right at my feet (I literally have to step over and around her the whole time). That part wasn't too hard to adjust to, dog cuddles are unparalled.

There were many other things during the first week and over time I came to learn about Blair. Like I said, it was difficult, but I wouldn't change it for a thing.

The Best Times

I'll be fully transparent here, Blair is the BEST dog. She's loving, cuddly, and aims to please with everything. BUT, she's no hero or athlete dog by any means. She's never saved a life or won a dog show competition. In fact, she'll probably find a way to do the opposite. But all her qualities combined make her amazing. And with those qualities, every day with her is the best possible time. ♥ ♥ ♥

The Scary Times

Now this section is where the meat of the content is. Blair has singlehandedly shaved a decade off my life. Whether it's things out of her control or things she willingly did, here are a couple of the MANY scary stories.

The Time She Ate A Boot

The week prior to Christmas 2022, I had been sick, and consequently, had been missing our daily walks, brain games, and mental training we did. I'm gone for work for 8 hours everyday, and on that Wednesday, I went to a Christmas dinner after work. Upon returning home, I walk in the door and immediately step on a piece of leather. When I turn on the light, I see my duck boots completely torn apart and scattered all over the floor. Upon inspection, I noticed that the pieces that were left did not add up to the whoel pair of boots. I took her to the vet first thing the next morning, and they came to the conclusion that she needed surgery ASAP. She had metal ringlets all through her stomach and no amount of induced vomiting could get them out. Few days of loopiness later, she was back to her normal self (minus the incision)and I have learned my leasson to never leave my shoes out when I leave!

The Time She Jumped the Fence...TWICE!

Imagine this: it's Sunday morning, you're half asleep and the dog woke you up to eat and go outside. You let them out into the backyard that you've let them roam solo in for many days. Suddenly, the neighbors little chihuahua starts causing a riot at the fence and your dog starts squaring up with it. Effortlessly she jumps the fence. At this point I went into panic mode. I had no shoes on, Blair is now no longer in the safe space of the yard, and I needed to go get her! I ran inside, grabbed shoes and became Superwoman in that moment. I flew through the backyard and launched myself over the fence with speed and strength that I didn't even know I had. Luckily, she was distracted by a frog and had been caught behind a trunk. I got her home, and since then have taken her out (in a fenced backyard!) on leash.

The Time She Had An Epulis

A couple months back, in April of 2023, I was leaving for work and noticed that Blair had a pink mass in her mouth. Upon inspection, I noticed her front bottom teeth were being pushed aside and a mass was growing between them. I called the vet ASAP and they brought her in for a check up and informed me that she had an epulis. An epulis is overgrowth of gum tissue. The vet said she needed surgery and likely she would get more growths in the future, which will require surgery as well. The biopsy came back benign and the epulis was removed. She now has 3 missing front teeth and I have learned to keep a closer eye on her teeth for any abnormalities!
